Why did the chicken cross the road?

Nicholas Winterhalter

Mar 3, 2025 12.5K 354

Once upon a time, in the quiet town of Featherbrook, there lived a chicken named Cluckles. Unlike the other chickens who spent their days pecking at the ground and clucking lazily in the barnyard, Cluckles had a dream—a dream of adventure, of seeing what lay beyond the road that divided the farm from the great unknown.

The other animals warned him about the dangers of the road. The wise old owl told tales of fast-moving monsters with glowing eyes that could flatten anything in their path. The grumpy goat scoffed, saying, "No chicken has ever crossed that road and lived to tell the tale!"

But Cluckles was determined. One night, under the silver glow of the moon, he trained in secret—practicing his dodges, flapping his wings for extra speed, and even studying the traffic patterns of the monstrous metal beasts that roared past every day. He knew that if he wanted to succeed, he had to be ready.

Then, the day came.

With a deep breath, Cluckles stepped forward, his heart pounding like a drum. The wind howled past him as he sprinted towards the edge of the farm, feathers ruffling in the cool morning breeze. He paused at the roadside, watching as the great metal beasts zoomed past with terrifying speed. Timing was everything.

He waited.

And then—an opening!

With all the strength in his tiny legs, Cluckles dashed forward. The ground vibrated beneath him as he dodged one beast, then another, barely escaping the wheels of doom. The world seemed to blur around him, but he did not stop. He would not stop.

Finally, with one final leap, he landed safely on the other side. The silence was deafening. He turned back, looking at the stunned farm animals who had gathered to witness his daring feat. The old owl’s beak hung open. The goat’s eyes were as wide as eggs. And then—

A cheer erupted from the farm!

Cluckles had done the impossible. He had crossed the road.

As he stood victorious, looking out into the horizon, he knew this was only the beginning. If he could conquer the road, then surely, he could conquer the world.

And that, dear reader, is why the chicken crossed the road.
